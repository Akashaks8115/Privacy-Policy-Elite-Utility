Privacy Policy

Last Updated: May 19, 2026

Welcome to Multi Tools ("we", "our", or "app"). We are highly committed to respecting and protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy document outlines how our mobile application operates and explicitly clarifies our practices regarding user data collection, usage, and security.

Core Commitment to Privacy

Multi Tools is a local, offline-first application. We do not operate cloud servers for core utilities, and we do not collect, upload, store, or share any personal information, files, or sensitive sensor metrics generated inside the application.

1. Information Collection & Usage

Since the application functions primarily as a local device utility suite, data processing is restricted entirely to your physical device. Below is a detailed breakdown of how individual tools interact with your device hardware:

A. On-Device File Processing Tools

  • Video to Audio Converter & Audio Trimmer: These features require read and write permissions to access your local multimedia storage. Media conversion is done entirely offline using standard on-device binaries. Your files are never transferred to external servers.
  • Document Scanner & Watermark Tool: Requires local file and camera system access to capture or load images. Final outputs (PDFs or watermarked images) are written strictly to your device's storage architecture.

B. Hardware Sensor-Driven Tools

  • GPS Speedometer: This tool utilizes your device's built-in Global Positioning System (GPS) hardware to determine velocity, max speed, and path distance. This coordinate data is computed on the fly and is never transmitted, broadcasted, or tracked remotely.
  • Heart Rate / BPM Monitor (Experimental): Utilizes camera stream frame buffers to track micro-fluctuations in blood volume intensity via light reflection. Pulse calculations happen in isolated system memory and are instantly cleared upon closing the session.
  • Compass, Bubble Level, & Metal Detector: These features stream raw hardware data directly from your device's magnetometer, accelerometer, and gyroscope sensors. Sensor telemetry is mapped purely onto visual UI states and is not logged anywhere.

2. Third-Party Advertising Frameworks

To support continuous development and keep the tool suite free for users, the app integrates the Google AdMob SDK for rendering advertisements when an active internet network interface is detected.

AdMob may dynamically collect and utilize certain non-personally identifiable identifiers. This processing is regulated under Google's respective policies and may include:

  • IP Addresses (for approximate geo-location sorting).
  • Unique device advertising identifiers (e.g., Google Advertising ID / AAID).
  • Diagnostic crash reporting logs and baseline network performance metrics.

3. Data Security & Storage Management

Because all data related to personal utility management—including notes, checklists, local configurations, and tool states—is retained solely within isolated application sandboxes (e.g., SharedPreferences or local databases), the security of your information relies directly on your physical device security. Uninstalling the application permanently deletes all locally indexed records instantly.

4. Children's Privacy Rights

Our app utilities do not deliberately target, gather data from, or profile children under the age of 13. Since zero persistent personal data is transmitted off the device to our infrastructure, we do not intentionally process or maintain parental-consent-restricted profiles.

We reserve the right to revise or modify this dynamic Privacy Policy declaration sequentially as new offline modules or underlying compliance baselines change. We recommend auditing this statement regularly through our official application distribution platform interfaces.
